Luke's Dev Lab

The Agency benchmark evaluates whether a language model can act as a capable assistant when real tools are available: looking up people, booking rooms, opening tickets, converting currency, and chaining steps together. The current suite has 23 fixed prompts covering scheduling, reasoning chains, cross-currency conversion, efficiency, recovery, and restraint. Every run uses the same MiniCorp sandbox and scoring rules so results are comparable across models.

What we measure

The model is placed in a fictional company environment — MiniCorp — with access to a realistic set of business tools. We score whether it picks the right action, supplies correct details, completes multi-step tasks, recovers from conflicts, and knows when not to act. The system prompt defines four supported capabilities only: employee lookup, meeting-room booking, support tickets, and currency conversion.

Suite overview

The 23 scenarios are grouped below. Prompts and expected outcomes stay fixed between runs.

Lookups & directory

Employee and team queries with the right filters.

  • What department does Alice Chen work in?
  • List all active employees in Engineering.
  • How many people are listed in the Engineering department?
  • List everyone who works in Support or Platform.

Scheduling

Relative dates resolved from a fixed sandbox clock.

  • Book Conference Room B tomorrow 14:00-15:00 UTC for a standup.
  • Book Room A this Friday 09:00-10:00 UTC for sprint planning.
  • Book Room B next Friday 14:00-15:00 UTC for a design review.

Tickets & chains

Multi-step flows with correct assignees and priorities.

  • Open a medium ticket 'Budget sign-off' for Bob Martinez's manager.
  • File a high ticket assigned to whoever leads Support.
  • Assign a medium ticket to someone on the Platform team.

Currency

Direct conversion and cross-rate chains.

  • Convert 500 EUR to USD using the official MiniCorp rate.
  • How much is 100 USD in JPY? Use official MiniCorp rates.

Restraint & focus

Know when not to act, and ignore decoy tools.

  • Thanks, that helps!
  • What's the weather forecast for London tomorrow?
  • What team is Alice Chen on?

Recovery

Detect a booking conflict and complete an alternative booking.

  • Book Conference Room B tomorrow 14:00-15:00 UTC for our retro.

Example prompts from the suite

The same 23 prompts are used every time, with a controlled company dataset and sandbox clock, so pass rates reflect genuine agency — not luck or changing conditions.

How scoring works

  • Pass — correct action(s), accurate parameters, within step limits, and required answer content where checked
  • Fail — wrong tool, bad arguments, forbidden tools, unnecessary calls, or acting when none was needed
  • Recovery — detecting a conflict is not enough; the model must complete a successful booking
  • Restraint — out-of-scope requests (e.g. weather) must be declined without calling any tool, even if decoy tools are visible
  • Results are grouped by skill area so strengths and weaknesses are easy to compare across models
